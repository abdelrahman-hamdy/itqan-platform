Three scheduling reports from teacher Shaymaa Al-Azab Under review

Reported 2026-05-06 · 3 students · awaiting academy admin decision on next steps

What was reported: Teacher شيماء العزب raised three concerns about her students' calendars. We investigated each one against the live database and the system logs, and the three reports turned out to have three different underlying situations — one is a real missing-session issue, one is a different real problem (an expired subscription), and one is a misunderstanding where the session is actually present.

Background — the 23 April cleanup, what it did, and why it matters here

Two of the three reports above trace back to the same event. Here it is in plain English:

What happened on 7 April 2026: our database was restored from a backup taken on 31 March, with the missing days replayed from the live transaction log. This is a normal recovery procedure, but in our case it left the database with side-effects: some students ended up with more scheduled sessions than their package paid for, and a small number of sessions appeared as exact duplicates (same teacher, same student, same time slot).
What happened on 23 April 2026: we ran a one-time cleanup script (authored and executed by the engineering team — not an automated scheduler) that fixed the recovery side-effects. In about one minute it removed the over-scheduled and duplicate sessions and recalculated the affected counters. The cleanup was needed and correct, but it ran silently — no email, no in-app notice to teachers — so the missing sessions only surface when a teacher looks at her calendar later. This is the underlying reason teacher Shaymaa is seeing "missing" sessions today.

What's the takeaway?

  • The cleanup itself was correct. It did exactly what it was supposed to do — remove future slots that the package didn't actually pay for, and de-duplicate sessions that the database recovery had double-stamped. Without it, students would have continued taking lessons they hadn't paid for, and the platform's counters would have stayed inconsistent.
  • It did NOT delete real lessons. Every already-attended lesson is still in the system. The only "completed" sessions removed were duplicate copies of lessons that already exist — the original/winner copy was kept in every case.
  • Teacher earnings are essentially intact. Future scheduled sessions never carry earnings (earnings are recorded only when a lesson is actually delivered), so the 152 future-slot removals had zero effect on what teachers will be paid. Of the 14 duplicate removals, only 2 earning rows were touched: 1 was a true duplicate (the winning copy still pays the teacher) and 1 left teacher #72 short by 50 SAR — recommended to reissue manually.
  • No student lost a paid lesson and no payments were reversed. Students whose "remaining balance" went down only lost the over-allocated future slots, not the package they paid for.
  • The communication was the failure, not the data work. Teachers weren't told their calendars would change, so missing sessions surfaced later as confused reports. Going forward, any cleanup of this kind will require a dry-run report and a per-teacher heads-up before any data is touched.

Quarterly & yearly subscriptions showing wrong next-payment date Resolved

Reported & resolved 2026-05-05 · 7 subscriptions corrected

What was happening: A student with a quarterly (3-month) subscription opened her circle page and saw that her next payment was due in roughly 1 month, not 3. After looking into it, the same problem was found on 6 other students, including one with a yearly subscription that was being treated as if it renewed every month.

Why it mattered

The actual subscription end date in the system was correct — only the date shown to the student in the "next payment" box was wrong. There was no risk of being billed early or charged twice, but it caused confusion and made it look like the platform did not honor the cycle the student had paid for.

What we fixed

Two places in the code that create subscriptions had a small flaw: when a student picked a quarterly or yearly plan, those places fell back to a 1-month cycle for the "next payment" date in certain cases. We changed both places to always use the chosen billing cycle (1, 3, or 12 months). We also corrected the 7 existing students whose subscriptions already had the wrong date stored.

Outcome: All 7 students now see the correct next-payment date. Future subscriptions cannot drift back into this state — the underlying calculation now goes through one shared, verified piece of code.

Tap payments captured but subscriptions stuck on "waiting for payment" Resolved

Reported & resolved 2026-05-05 · 5 subscriptions restored · 850 SAR captured

What was happening: Five students paid through the Tap payment gateway and saw a blank/white page after checkout. When they returned to the app, their subscription page still showed "في انتظار الدفع" ("waiting for payment"). The money was taken correctly by Tap — only the subscription on our side did not unlock.

Why it mattered

These students paid for a service they could not use. Without action they would have either had to pay a second time or open a refund request, and the academy would have looked unreliable.

What we fixed

A code change made earlier in the week introduced a small mismatch: when a brand-new student paid for the first time, the system attached the payment to the wrong "billing cycle slot" inside the subscription. The activation step then thought there was nothing to do and exited silently, leaving the subscription marked as unpaid even though Tap had captured the money.

We corrected the activation logic so it only short-circuits for subscriptions that are already active, and we manually re-activated the 5 stuck subscriptions so each student got immediate access to what they paid for.

Outcome: All 5 subscriptions are now active and paid. Every student got the service they paid for. No refunds were needed. New Tap payments since the fix are activating normally.
